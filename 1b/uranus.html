The Sideways Planet:
Uranus, the seventh planet from the Sun, is known for its unique axial tilt, causing it to rotate on its side.
An Ice Giant:
Unlike the gas giants, Jupiter and Saturn, Uranus is classified as an ice giant, primarily composed of water, methane, and ammonia.
A Pale Blue Hue:
Uranus's atmosphere, rich in methane, gives it a distinctive pale blue color.
Faint Rings:
Uranus has a system of faint rings, discovered in 1977.
Numerous Moons:
The planet has 27 known moons, many of which are named after characters from the works of William Shakespeare and Alexander Pope.
Extreme Cold:
As the farthest planet from the Sun, Uranus experiences extremely cold temperatures, making it one of the coldest places in the solar system.
A Mysterious Interior:
The interior of Uranus remains largely unknown, with scientists still working to understand its composition and structure.
