Venus, often called Earth's "sister planet," is the second planet from the Sun and the brightest natural object in Earth's night sky after the Moon.
A Fiery Hell:
Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system, with surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead. This extreme heat is due to a runaway greenhouse effect, where the thick atmosphere traps heat from the Sun.
A Toxic Atmosphere:
Venus's atmosphere is primarily composed of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid. This dense atmosphere creates a crushing pressure at the surface, about 92 times that of Earth's.
Slow Rotation:
Venus rotates very slowly on its axis, taking about 243 Earth days to complete one rotation. This slow rotation, combined with its thick atmosphere, contributes to the planet's extreme heat.
Retrograde Rotation:
Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most other planets, spinning clockwise when viewed from above the North Pole.
Volcanic Activity:
Venus has a volcanic surface, with numerous volcanoes, some of which may still be active. These volcanoes release gases into the atmosphere, contributing to the planet's extreme conditions.
A Lack of Moons:
Unlike Earth, Venus has no moons orbiting it.
A Potential for Life?
While Venus's surface is inhospitable to life as we know it, some scientists speculate that life might exist in the planet's upper atmosphere, where conditions are less extreme.
