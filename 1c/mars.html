The Red Planet, Mars, is a cold, dusty world with a thin atmosphere.
A Rusty Landscape:
Mars' distinctive red color comes from iron oxide, or rust, which covers much of its surface.
Giant Canyons and Volcanoes:
The planet boasts the largest canyon in the solar system, Valles Marineris, and the tallest volcano, Olympus Mons.
Polar Ice Caps:
Mars has polar ice caps composed of frozen water and carbon dioxide, which grow and shrink with the seasons.
A Thin Atmosphere:
Mars' atmosphere is much thinner than Earth's, primarily composed of carbon dioxide.
Two Tiny Moons:
Mars has two small, irregularly shaped moons: Phobos and Deimos.
A Potential for Past Life:
Scientists believe that Mars may have once had liquid water on its surface, raising the possibility of past microbial life.
A Target for Exploration:
Mars is a popular target for robotic exploration, with rovers like Curiosity and Perseverance studying its geology and searching for signs of past habitability.
